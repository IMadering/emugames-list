The Psion Series 3 range of personal digital assistants were made by Psion PLC. The four main variants are the Psion Series 3 (1991), the Psion Series 3a (1993), the Psion Series 3c (1996), and the Psion Series 3mx (1998).
Acorn Computers sold renamed versions of the Psion Series 3 and 3a marketed as the Acorn Pocket Book and Acorn Pocket Book II.

One unique feature of the Psion Series 3 software package was a built-in programming language, Organiser then Open Programming Language (OPL), which enabled users to create their own applications that ran and looked just as system programs.

Technical Specifications - Psion Series 3 (Introduced 1991)
CPU: NEC V30H 3.84 MHz
RAM: 128 KB / 256 KB
ROM: 256 KB
Screen: Monochrome LCD with resolution of 240x80
Sound: Internal piezo beeper
OS: SIBO 16-bit (EPOC)

Technical Specifications - Psion Series 3a (Introduced 1993)
CPU: NEC V30H 7.68 MHz
RAM: 256 KB - 2 MB
ROM: 1 MB
Screen: Monochrome LCD with resolution of 480x160
Sound: Internal membrane speaker, built-in microphone
OS: SIBO 16-bit (EPOC)

Technical Specifications - Psion Series 3c (Introduced 1996)
CPU: NEC V30H 7.68 MHz
RAM: 1 MB / 2 MB
ROM: 1 MB / 2 MB
Screen: Monochrome LCD with resolution of 480x160
Sound: Internal membrane speaker, built-in microphone
OS: SIBO 16-bit (EPOC)

Technical Specifications - Psion Siena (Introduced 1996)
CPU: NEC V30H 7.68 MHz
RAM: 512 KB / 1 MB
ROM: 512 KB / 1 MB
Screen: Monochrome LCD with resolution of 240x160
Sound: Internal piezo beeper
OS: SIBO 16-bit (EPOC)

Technical Specifications - Psion Series 3mx (Introduced 1998)
CPU: NEC V30MX 27.6 MHz
RAM: 2 MB
ROM: 2 MB
Screen: Monochrome LCD with resolution of 480x160
Sound: Internal membrane speaker, built-in microphone
OS: SIBO 16-bit (EPOC)
