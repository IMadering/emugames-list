The V.Smile (full name: V.Smile TV Learning System) is a unique educational game console developed by VTech specifically for preschool and early school-aged children (typically 3 to 7-9 years old).
Manufacturer: VTech (a Hong Kong-based company specializing in educational electronics).
Release Date: First launched in 2004.
Purpose: Education and development (edutainment). Games focus on teaching letters, numbers, spelling, logic, and other core educational concepts.

The V.Smile was a highly successful console for its segment, selling over 11 million units. It served as an "intermediate step" between traditional toys and full-fledged video game consoles, offering parents a compromise between entertainment and education, often using popular licensed characters (Disney, Nick Jr., etc.).

Technical Specifications
CPU: Sunplus SPG200, clock speed ~27 MHz
Graphics: 2D sprite-based graphics. 320x240 pixels.
Media: V.Smile Smartridges (based on ROM flash memory)
Audio: Mono / Stereo

2nd Generation (2007)

V.Smile Motion (2007) (also known as V.Smile V-Motion) is a revision and update of the original V.Smile system. It was released by VTech around 2007-2008. In terms of the basic "hardware" (processor and graphics), the V.Smile Motion remained very close to the original V.Smile, using the same architecture to ensure backward compatibility with older games.
